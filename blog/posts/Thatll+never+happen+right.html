That'll never happen, right?

We've all thought this, at one time or another. It's a fairly easy way to procrastinate; why do something "meaningless?" I've been burned recently, as you might guess. It started in November: a tied football game.

Ties in the NFL are rare: only three have occurred this century. As such, the support for ties in NFLScoreBot was, at best, halfhearted. In English, everything was fine; the summaries weren't particularly elegant, but it worked. As I found that day, I had never actually bothered to do the translation work for French output. I got it working, but I've never implemented a feature faster.

I got burned again this month. The odds of two pieces of information being scored at the exact same value is ridiculously low. It happened, and I found a bug in my code that led to information being associated with the wrong team.

The third time it got me? There have been six double-overtime games in NFL history; one of them was this week. Thankfully, it was a quick fix.

Laziness really does catch up to you. Some of these problems were things I would be unlikely to catch; the second problem came down to a single use of < instead of <=.

The others, though, were things I knew about. I though I could get away with them for just one more season. Then I'd fix it.

I couldn't.
